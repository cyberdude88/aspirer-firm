## 1. Purpose and Scope of This Policy

This policy explains how confidentiality works in the coaching relationship between Aspirer Firm and its clients. It is intended to set clear expectations before any paid engagement begins.

This document defines the boundaries of confidentiality for discovery calls, paid 1:1 coaching, co-founder pair sessions, email and messaging exchanges, scheduling communications, and related service-delivery interactions.

This document is not a therapy informed-consent form, not a HIPAA notice, and not a promise of legal privilege. Aspirer Firm provides non-clinical mindset coaching, not psychotherapy or medical treatment, and this policy should be read together with the Terms of Service / Coaching Agreement and the Privacy Policy.

This policy applies to all Aspirer Firm clients globally. Additional provisions for EU/EEA and UK clients appear in Section 8 because those clients may provide special category personal data under GDPR and UK GDPR.

## 2. The Coaching Relationship Is Not a Therapeutic Relationship

Aspirer Firm provides mindset coaching for entrepreneurs and operators. Services are not therapy, diagnosis, treatment, crisis response, or emergency mental health care.

Marie Cook is a Licensed Mental Health Professional, but in this engagement she is acting in a non-clinical coaching capacity. That distinction matters because the coaching relationship is not the same as a therapist-patient relationship.

No therapist-patient privilege, psychotherapist privilege, or similar clinical evidentiary privilege is created by this coaching relationship unless applicable law expressly provides otherwise in a specific context. Clients should not assume that a court, regulator, or third party will treat coaching communications as privileged in the way clinical therapy records may sometimes be treated.

Confidentiality in this relationship is therefore contractual and professional. Aspirer Firm commits to handling client information carefully, discreetly, and consistently with this policy, but does not promise absolute secrecy or clinical privilege.

## 3. What Information Is Treated as Confidential

Subject to the limits described in this policy, Aspirer Firm treats as confidential information that a client shares in connection with the coaching relationship, including session content, discovery-call content, written correspondence, scheduling context, billing-related communications, intake details, and information shared through phone, video, email, social media direct message, booking forms, or similar channels.

Confidential information may include business context, decision-making patterns, relationship concerns, emotional disclosures, stress responses, wellbeing concerns, and other personal or professional matters discussed during or around the engagement.

This definition is intended to be broad enough to protect the relationship while remaining honest about operational realities. Information that a client independently makes public, instructs Aspirer Firm in writing to disclose, or that Aspirer Firm must disclose under law or professional duty is not protected in the same way.

## 5. Crisis Protocol

Aspirer Firm is not a crisis service, emergency service, therapy practice, or substitute for clinical mental health care. Clients should not use Aspirer Firm as their point of contact in an emergency or urgent psychiatric situation.

If a client appears to be in crisis, expresses suicidality, appears unable to use coaching safely, or otherwise appears to need clinical care or emergency intervention, Marie Cook may pause or end the coaching interaction and direct the client to appropriate clinical, emergency, or community resources.

Where reasonably necessary to facilitate care or protect safety, Marie Cook may share information with emergency responders, crisis resources, a welfare-check agency, a licensed clinician, or another appropriate person or entity. Only the information reasonably necessary for that purpose should be shared.

Clients acknowledge that in the United States they should call 988 for the Suicide and Crisis Lifeline or 911 for emergency services when immediate danger exists.

## 6. Co-Founder Pair Sessions — Special Handling

In a co-founder pair session, information shared in the session is not confidential as between the two participating founders. Each founder is present for the full conversation and should assume that material discussed in the room is shared with both participants.

As between the pair and the outside world, Aspirer Firm will still treat the session as confidential subject to the limits in this policy, including mandatory disclosures, crisis intervention, legal compulsion, consultation, and operational processing.

If one founder later books a solo session, Aspirer Firm may consider context learned during prior joint sessions in order to coach safely and coherently. However, Marie Cook will not act as a secret-keeper for one founder inside a joint coaching container if doing so would distort the integrity of the pair work.

If one founder requests a solo conversation related to the pair engagement, Marie Cook may hear the concern privately, but may require that material relevant to the pair's work be raised in a later joint session before continuing the pair engagement. Aspirer Firm does not guarantee that information disclosed privately by one founder can be permanently withheld from the joint process if it materially affects the shared coaching work, safety, or ethical functioning of the engagement.

Where a separate solo engagement is created for one founder, Aspirer Firm will clarify in writing whether that solo engagement is separate from, adjunct to, or incompatible with the joint engagement.

## 8. GDPR Article 9 — Special Category Data (EU/EEA and UK Clients)

EU/EEA and UK clients may disclose information about mental state, emotions, wellbeing, stress, relationships, and similar matters in coaching sessions and related communications. Even in a non-clinical coaching setting, that information may constitute special category personal data under Article 9 GDPR and UK GDPR.

Aspirer Firm therefore treats this type of information as special category data when applicable.

For EU/EEA and UK clients, Aspirer Firm's primary lawful basis for ordinary personal data needed to deliver the service is Article 6(1)(b), performance of a contract, and in some cases Article 6(1)(a), consent, for specific optional processing activities.

For special category data, Aspirer Firm relies on the client's explicit consent under Article 9(2)(a) for the processing of information the client chooses to disclose in connection with coaching.

This explicit consent is obtained through the client's signed or electronically recorded acknowledgment of this Confidentiality Policy before the first paid session.

EU/EEA and UK clients may withdraw that explicit consent at any time by writing to marie.cook@aspirerfirm.com. Withdrawal will not affect the lawfulness of processing carried out before withdrawal.

If explicit consent is withdrawn, Aspirer Firm will stop future processing of special category data for coaching purposes to the extent legally required and operationally possible. In practice, withdrawal of consent may make it impossible for Aspirer Firm to continue the coaching engagement.

Even after withdrawal, Aspirer Firm may retain limited records where retention is necessary for legal claims, professional record-keeping, insurance, fraud prevention, tax compliance, licensing defense, or other legally supportable retention purposes. Attorney review is required to confirm the final retention language and lawful bases for retained records.

EU/EEA and UK clients may have the right to request access, rectification, erasure, restriction of processing, data portability, and to object to certain processing, subject to legal limitations. Erasure may not apply where Aspirer Firm must retain records for legal, ethical, licensing, insurance, or defense purposes. Further detail appears in the Privacy Policy.

EU/EEA and UK clients also have the right to lodge a complaint with the supervisory authority in the country of their habitual residence, place of work, or place of the alleged infringement. Examples include CNIL in France, BfDI in Germany, Garante in Italy, AEPD in Spain, the Data Protection Commission in Ireland, and the ICO in the United Kingdom.

Questions remain for attorney review regarding whether Aspirer Firm requires an EU representative under GDPR Article 27 and whether any DPO appointment analysis is required given the volume and regularity of Article 9 data processing.
